Analyzing the glitz and glamor of Hollywood would be incomplete without examining the enchanting journey of the remarkable Robin Wright! Born on April 8, 1966, this talented actress has left an indelible mark on the world of cinema. From commanding roles in blockbusters to critically acclaimed performances, she has proven her versatility time and again. In this great article, we dive into the realm of box office success and unveil Robin Wright's top 10 highest-grossing movies, ranked for your enjoyment. As we celebrate her illustrious career, we’ll revel in her iconic portrayal of Jenny Curran in Forrest Gump and her captivating role as Antiope in Wonder Woman. Join us as we embark on a cinematic journey like no other with the iconic acting prowess of Robin Wright.

Moneyball - 2011 ($111.3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the highly praised sports drama Moneyball, produced by Brad Pitt and directed by Bennett Miller, Robin Wright gives a memorable performance as Sharon, an independent and devoted ex-wife. The movie is based on the actual account of the Oakland Athletics baseball team and their creative selection strategies. Wright's interpretation gives her character complexity and refinement, giving the story more emotional weight. With a worldwide box office haul of $111.3 million, Moneyball had a successful run.

Wonder Woman 1984 - 2020 ($166.3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Robin Wright reprises her role as the fierce Amazonian warrior Antiope in the superhero sequel Wonder Woman 1984, directed by Patty Jenkins and produced by Charles Roven. Her character plays a vital role in shaping the backstory and inner strength of the movie's titular hero, Wonder Woman. Wright's commanding presence and skillful portrayal add depth to the action-packed storyline, contributing to the movie's success and its worldwide box office haul of $166.3 million.

Beowulf - 2007 ($195 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Portraying Queen Wealthow, a powerful and alluring figure in an ancient kingdom in the epic fantasy movie Beowulf, directed by Robert Zemeckis and produced by Steve Starkey, Robin Wright proved her versatility in acting. The stunning movie captivated audiences with its mesmerizing motion-capture animation and thrilling storytelling. Wright's performance as the regal queen brings elegance and complexity to the role, making her a standout in the mythical tale. Beowulf grossed an impressive $195 million worldwide.

Everest - 2015 ($221.2 Million)

Via Flickr/Three Penny Press

In the gripping survival drama Everest, directed by Baltasar Kormákur and produced by Tim Bevan, Robin Wright takes on the role of Peach Weathers, a determined and resilient mountaineer. The movie chronicles the true story of a tragic expedition to Mount Everest, showcasing the dangers and triumphs faced by the climbers. Wright's performance captures the emotional intensity and strength of her character, adding a layer of humanity to the movie. Everest reached new heights at the box office, grossing $221.2 million worldwide.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo - 2011 ($239.3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Robin Wright brings depth and intrigue to the role of Erika Berger, a powerful magazine editor in the thrilling adaptation of Stieg Larsson's novel, The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo, directed by David Fincher and produced by Scott Rudin. Her character's intelligence and complex relationship with the film's protagonist contributes to the intricate web of mystery and suspense. Wright's captivating performance elevates the movie, which grossed $239.3 million worldwide.

Unbreakable - 2000 ($248 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In M. Night Shyamalan's psychological superhero thriller Unbreakable, produced by Barry Mendel, Robin Wright shines as Audrey Dunne, a compassionate and supportive wife. Her character provides a grounding presence for the movie's enigmatic protagonist. Wright's portrayal adds emotional depth to the story, enhancing the movie's exploration of identity and extraordinary abilities. Unbreakable achieved solid success at the box office, grossing $248 million worldwide.

Blade Runner 2049 - 2017 ($257.7 Million)

Via Flickr/Stan

Wright portrays Lieutenant Joshi, a determined no-nonsense officer in the stunning science fiction sequel Blade Runner 2049, directed by Denis Villeneuve and produced by Andrew A. Kosove. Robin’s character plays a crucial role in the movie's dystopian world, unraveling a complex web of mysteries. Her commanding performance adds gravitas to the narrative, contributing to its success as it grossed $257.7 million globally.

Disney's A Christmas Carol - 2009 ($315.7 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Directed by Robert Zemeckis and produced by Steve Starkey, Robin Wright lends her talents to multiple roles, including Fan and Belle in the enchanting holiday movie Disney's A Christmas Carol. Her performances capture the warmth and magic of the Christmas spirit, adding a touch of nostalgia and heart to the movie. Wright's portrayal resonated with audiences, contributing to the movie's box office success of $315.7 million worldwide.

Forrest Gump - 1994 ($679.8 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the beloved classic Forrest Gump, directed by Robert Zemeckis and produced by Wendy Finerman, Robin Wright delivers a moving performance as Jenny Curran, the love interest of the titular character. Her portrayal captures the complexity of Jenny's journey and brings depth to their enduring connection. The movie's sweeping storytelling struck a chord with audiences, propelling it to earn an impressive $679.8 million worldwide.

Wonder Woman - 2017 ($817.6 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Robin Wright portrays Antiope, an Amazonian warrior who trains and mentors the movie's protagonist in the groundbreaking superhero movie Wonder Woman, directed by Patty Jenkins and produced by Charles Roven. Her character's strength and wisdom shape the hero's journey, adding layers of empowerment and inspiration. Wright's commanding presence and dynamic performance contributed to the movie's immense success as it went on to gross $817.6 million at the worldwide box office.
