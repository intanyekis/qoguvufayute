Will Ferrell invites Cristiano Ronaldo to join ex-Real Madrid teammate Gareth Bale at LAFC as Manche

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Cristiano Ronaldo's explosive interview with Piers Morgan has left many fans asking: 'Who on earth would take him now?'

Well, we have the answer and it is your favourite Christmas elf, Will Ferrell.

The Hollywood actor, who also happens to part-own MLS franchise Los Angeles FC, would have no problem taking five-time Ballon d'Or winner Ronaldo at his club.

But the Portugal legend may have to accept a pay cut on his £500,000-a-week salary at Old Trafford.

"Always come to LA! You're welcome," Ferrell told talkSPORT.

"We can't pay you that much, but there's really nice golf courses and beaches.

"Just try it!"

Speaking of golf courses, a move to LA would represent a reunion for Ronaldo with ex-Real Madrid teammate Gareth Bale.

Most read in Football

The Welshman recently helped inspire LAFC to the MLS Cup with a dramatic last-gasp equaliser in the final.

But Bale is not the only friend Ronaldo has in America.

According to The Sun, David Beckham's MLS franchise Inter Miami are also interested in taking Ronaldo off United's hands.

Miami are managed by Ronaldo's ex-Red Devils teammate Phil Neville - and a reunion between the pair would be quite funny given what the striker just said about Phil's brother, Gary.

Ronaldo declared he is not friends with Gary Neville after recent criticism and broke the Man United legend's heart by insisting they will never go for dinner.

The 37-year-old also made a number of astonishing complaints about his difficulties at Old Trafford, hitting out at manager Erik ten Hag, the Glazer family and Wayne Rooney.
