Bajaj Pulsar NS 600: Mileage and Top Speed

The Bajaj Pulsar NS 600 is expected to have a mileage of 21 to 23 km/l and a top speed of 160-170 km/h, which is quite impressive. This performance positions it as a competitive sports bike in the 600cc market segment.

Bajaj Pulsar NS 600: Brakes and Tyres

Bajaj Pulsar NS 600 is to be fitted with dual hydraulic disc brake with 4-piston callipers and its size is 300 mm. and the bike is to be fitted with single hydraulic disc brake with single piston callipers and its size is 230 mm. 

Both the tyres fitted in the bike are tubeless and the size of the tyre is 17 inches. 

Bajaj Pulsar NS 600: Overview

The Bajaj Pulsar NS 600 is designed to be a dynamic streetfighter with minimum bodywork, showcasing exposed mechanics as well as muscular look. It promises an impressive and sturdy stance, emphasized by a robust fuel tank and intricately designed body panels. The bike is constructed on a tubular steel perimeter frame, and is expected to be known for its excellent stability and handling properties.

For suspension, the Pulsar NS 600 will incorporate a high-quality system, striking a perfect balance between comfort and easy handling. This includes inverted big piston forks at the front and a gas-charged mono-shock absorber with adjustable preload at the rear. Safety and control are further enhanced by the dual disc brakes on the front and a single disc brake on the rear.

At its heart, the Pulsar NS 600 will house a dual-cylinder, four-stroke, liquid-cooled engine and four valves per cylinder. This engine will be paired with a 6-speed constant mesh gearbox. The inclusion of a slipper clutch system will mitigate the risk of rear-wheel lockup during rapid downshifts, thereby boosting stability and safety. It will feature an advanced electronic fuel injection system for efficient fuel delivery, optimizing both performance and emissions. 

Additionally, a bi-directional quick-shifter will enable clutchless upshifts for seamless and faster gear transitions. The bike will also offer various riding modes, allowing riders to tailor its performance to suit diverse riding environments.

The bike will ride on 17-inch alloy wheels, fitted with high-performance radial tires, and will feature split seats for a more engaging, sporty posture.
