Julia Ioffe Education

Julia was born in Russia but later at the age of 7, her family relocated to the United States. They emigrated as refugees escaping anti-Semitism in the Soviet Union. Her family later settled in Columbia, Maryland where Julia attended Beth Tfiloh Dehan Community School and graduated in 2001.

She proceeded to Princeton University where she majored in Soviet History and Russian Literature. Her last university project which was the study of the Soviet connection to the Spanish speaking world won Julia an award with Davis Prize and also received a cash grant of $6,000. During her time in the university, Julia was the vice president of the Princeton Israel Public Affairs Committee.

Julia Ioffe CNN

Julia featured in ‘The Lead with Jake Tapper’ an afternoon early evening newscast hosted by Jake Tapper on CNN stating that President Donald Trump has radicalized so many people than ISIS ever did, pointing a 60% increase in antisemitism during 2017.

Her statement received pushback from David Urban and Mona Charen, her fellow panelists. Nevertheless, she later apologized for her statement which she termed as ‘Hyperbole’ during the broadcast and also on twitter. Trump, in an interview with Laura Ingraham on Fox News, called Julia some kind of a sick woman referring to her comments about him.

Julia Ioffe Politico

Julia in May 2016 became a contributing writer at Politico, an American political opinion company based in Virginia. She is also the first journalist to profile Steven Miller, Trump’s policy and speechwriter. Ioffe also wrote an article about ‘Cater Page’ an American petroleum industry consultant and former foreign policy advisor to Donald Trump during the 2016 presidential elections, who has had many years of experience investing in Russia and the energy sector.

Ioffe, in December 2016, issued a tweet directed to Donald Trump suggesting that he was involved in a sexual relationship with his daughter, for which after being blasted she later apologized by deleting and expressing the tweet as ‘offensive and tasteless. Julia was eventually fired from Politico. The Atlantic reported that it was hiring Ioffe to tackle topics on national security, foreign policy, and politics, with an editor in chief Jeffrey Goldberg describing her as “an enthusiastic reporter”.

Julia Ioffe Atlantic

Julia found out that “even if Trump’s older son mostly ignored the endless messages from WikiLeaks, he at some point appears to have acted on its pleas and shared that information with Donald Trump’s senior campaign officials”.

Julia was also the first to receive the whole e-mail correspondence between Trump’s campaign chief Paul Manafort and Oleg Deripaska, a Russian oligarch with close ties to the Kremlin.
