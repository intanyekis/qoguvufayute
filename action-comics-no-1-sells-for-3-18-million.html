Action Comics No. 1 Sells For $3.18 Million

September 2024 · 3 minute read

Action Comics No. 1 from 1938 is widely regarded as among the most influential graphic books of all time. Jerry Siegel and Joe Shuster present the universe to Superman, the figure who kickstarted the superhero genre. The cover of this specific edition of the DC Comics publication is known as "the Rocket Copy" because it has a jet stamp placed there by the 13-year-old who bought the edition from a newsstand in 1938. Heritage Auctions handled the auction. Thursday's Rocket Copy auction follows the $3.6 million sales of Amazing Fantasy No. 15, the very first debut of Spider-Man, in September 2021, and two other versions of Action Comics No. 1. The CGC has assigned a condition rating of 6.0 to the Rocket Copy.
